A practical, hands-on guide to the Specter API.

Whether you're a developer building an integration, a data scientist enriching a model, or a product manager exploring what's possible, you'll find what you need here. Got a question, hit a snag, or want to share feedback? Email us at [support@tryspecter.com](mailto:support@tryspecter.com).

Let's get started.

## Getting access

Request an API key from the [API Console](https://app.tryspecter.com/settings/api-console). We approve requests within one business day, and you'll get a confirmation email the moment your key is ready.

## Choosing your plan

There are two ways to use the Specter API. Which one you need comes down to whether you just want to enrich records you already have, or you want full access to Specter's databases and signals.

### Enrichment API

Streamlined enrichment for teams who don't need the full Specter platform or universal search. You give us a list of companies or people, say from your CRM or dealflow, and we hand back Specter's data on them. It runs on two endpoints: enrich companies and enrich people. No web app seat required.

For example, a growth team has 2,000 leads sitting in their CRM. They send us the domains and person identifiers, and get back funding rounds, headcount growth, and key executive profiles appended to each record.

### Full API

Comprehensive programmatic access to Specter's data and workflow features. You get the full Company and People databases, the proprietary Talent Signals and Interest Signals feeds, and workflow automation: create and save searches and lists, pull all their results programmatically, and subscribe to new matches as they appear. Verified emails and similar companies are in there too, with more endpoints added on an ongoing basis.

Using the Full API needs at least one seat on the Specter web app. That's what lets you build and manage your searches and lists in the interface, then pull all their results through the API.

For example, a fund tracks AI startups across Europe that are raising, watches talent move from Google into those companies, and pulls founder email addresses. They save those searches, subscribe to updates, and join company, people and signal data straight into their internal dealflow pipeline.
